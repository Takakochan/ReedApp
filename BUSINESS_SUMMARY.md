## 🎯 Key Business Opportunities

### 1. **Community Features** (Diagnostic, Not Competitive)

**"How Am I Doing?" Dashboard** — The Strava Move for Reed Makers:
```
Your Stats vs. Community:
- Your avg quality: 3.8 ⭐ (Community: 3.4)
- You're in top 28% of oboists—but remember:
  • Variation is normal in reed making
  • Different styles produce different results
  • This reflects outcomes, not absolute quality

Most popular brand in your region: Rigotti
💡 Your approach is working. Consider trying Glotin to experiment.
```

**Why This Works:**
- Musicians want self-awareness, not just rankings
- Diagnostic framing prevents interpretation anxiety
- Creates engagement and retention
- Natural upsell to Premium
- Builds community feeling without toxic competition

### 2. **Data as a Product** (Ethical Version)

**Critical Strategy**: First clients buy trust in your lens, not spreadsheets. Early reports = **ethnography with numbers**. Once manufacturers trust your understanding of the craft, APIs and subscriptions become obvious.

**Sell to Cane Manufacturers:**
- Quarterly Industry Report: $2,500 (narrative + data)
- Custom Research: $5,000-$15,000
- Annual Subscription: $20,000/year
- API Access: $500/month (after trust established)

**What They Get:**
- Aggregated market insights (told as stories)
- Product performance data (contextualized)
- Regional preferences
- Quality correlations
- Trend analysis
- Your lens on the craft (this is the real product)

**What They DON'T Get:**
- Individual user data
- Personal information
- Raw database access
- Identifiable information

### 3. **Network Effects** (Density Over Virality)

More users = Better insights = More value = More users

**But here's the key**: You need **density**, not virality. A few thousand serious users generate more meaningful insight than 50,000 casual ones. This aligns beautifully with a niche, professional craft.

This creates a **competitive moat** that's hard to replicate — and it scales quietly!
