Understanding Reed Parameters

Each parameter affects reed performance in specific ways. Understanding these relationships helps you make targeted adjustments to achieve your desired tone and response.

Physical Measurements

Diameter (Cane Width)

The outer diameter of the cane tube, typically measured before splitting. Diameter directly affects resistance and tone color.

  • Oboe: 10-12mm (most common: 10.5-11mm)
  • Bassoon: 24-26mm (most common: 25mm)
  • English Horn: 13-15mm

Impact: Larger diameter = more resistance, darker tone. Smaller diameter = less resistance, brighter tone. Diameter also affects the vibrating surface area and harmonic spectrum.

Thickness (Gouge Thickness)

The thickness at the center of the cane after gouging, measured with a dial indicator or micrometer.

  • Oboe: 0.55-0.60mm (tolerance: ±0.01mm)
  • Bassoon: 0.90-1.10mm
  • English Horn: 0.65-0.70mm

Impact: Thicker gouge = more resistant, darker sound, better low register. Thinner gouge = easier to play, brighter, better high register response. Final scraping compensates for initial gouge thickness.

Hardness

The rigidity of the cane fibers, assessed by touch or flexure testing. Hardness is influenced by cane maturity, growing conditions, and storage.

  • Soft: Easy blowing, quick response, may lack stability and projection
  • Medium: Balanced response and resistance, suitable for most players
  • Hard: High resistance, brilliant tone, excellent projection, requires strong embouchure

Selection tip: Match hardness to your embouchure strength and desired sound. Beginners often benefit from medium-soft cane; professionals may prefer medium-hard for projection.

Flexibility

How much the reed blades bend under light pressure. Flexibility affects articulation speed and dynamic range.

  • Flexible: Quick response, wide dynamics, may be unstable in pitch
  • Medium: Balanced articulation and stability
  • Stiff: Slower response, stable pitch, narrower dynamic range

Technical note: Flexibility is determined by fiber orientation, moisture content, and scrape profile. The tip area flexibility is most critical for response.

Density

The mass-to-volume ratio of the cane fibers, measured as g/cm³. ReedManage calculates this automatically from dry mass and wet mass using the formula: Density = M₁ / (M₁ + M₂)

  • High density (0.75-0.85): Durable, stable, projects well, slower break-in
  • Medium density (0.65-0.75): Balanced longevity and responsiveness
  • Low density (0.55-0.65): Responsive, bright, less durable

Measurement protocol: Measure M₁ (dry mass) after 24 hours at 20°C and 50% humidity. Measure M₂ (wet mass) after 2 minutes soaking. Density correlates with lignin content and cell wall thickness.

Equipment Parameters

Shaper

The template that defines the reed's lateral profile and overall shape. Different shapers produce distinct tonal characteristics.

Popular oboe shapers: Chiarugi 2 (narrow, focused), RDG 1 (medium width, balanced), Mack+ (wide, darker tone). Shaper tip width affects the sweet spot and harmonic balance.

Popular bassoon shapers: Rieger 1A, Herzberg, Fox 2. Shaper profile affects throat resistance and tonal complexity.

Gouging Machine

The device that removes bark and shapes the cane's thickness profile. Bed diameter and blade curve affect the final gouge.

Key settings: Bed diameter (typically 9.5-10.5mm for oboe), blade radius, and side thickness determine the thickness curve. Track which machine you use to identify consistency issues.

Environmental Factors

Weather Impact on Reed Performance

Temperature and humidity significantly affect reed behavior through hygroscopic changes in cane fibers. ReedManage automatically logs weather data to help you understand these correlations.

Temperature Effects:

  • Cold (<15°C): Reeds play sharp, stiffer response
  • Moderate (15-25°C): Optimal performance range
  • Warm (>25°C): Reeds play flat, softer feel

Humidity Effects:

  • Dry (<40%): Reeds harden, crack risk increases
  • Moderate (40-60%): Stable performance
  • Humid (>60%): Reeds soften, water log faster

Data analysis tip: After logging 20+ reeds, review your statistics to identify which cane hardness works best in your typical climate conditions.

Quality Assessment

Rating Your Reeds

Consistent quality ratings enable pattern recognition in your analytics. Rate reeds on a 1-5 scale based on these criteria:

  • 5 Stars: Professional performance quality - stable pitch, even response across registers, beautiful tone, excellent endurance (concert ready)
  • 4 Stars: Very good - minor adjustments needed, suitable for most performances
  • 3 Stars: Good for practice - functional but has limitations in tone or response
  • 2 Stars: Usable for warm-up - significant issues but can produce sound
  • 1 Star: Failed reed - unusable, learning experience only

Track multiple impressions: Rate reeds after initial play-in (1st impression), after break-in (2nd impression), and after extended use (3rd impression) to track longevity.

Best Practices for Data Collection

1.

Consistency is key: Use the same measurement techniques and rating criteria for all reeds. Calibrate your measurements with a micrometer and always measure at the same location.

2.

Document everything: Include detailed notes about scraping technique, adjustment strategies, and performance contexts. Future you will thank present you.

3.

Track failures too: Failed reeds provide valuable data. Understanding what doesn't work is as important as knowing what does.

4.

Use batch entry efficiently: When making multiple reeds from the same cane batch, use batch entry to quickly log shared parameters (brand, harvest year, equipment).

5.

Export regularly: Download your data monthly as backup. Use Excel export for custom analysis or CSV for compatibility with statistical software (R, Python).

6.

Review statistics quarterly: Set a reminder to review your analytics every 3 months to adjust your reed-making approach based on objective data.

Advanced Tip: Scientific Approach

Treat your reed-making like a controlled experiment. When testing a new variable (different shaper, cane brand, or thickness):

  1. Keep all other variables constant
  2. Make at least 5-6 reeds with the new parameter to account for natural variation
  3. Compare average quality ratings to your baseline
  4. Use ReedManage's correlation analysis to quantify the impact

This systematic approach accelerates skill development and leads to consistent, reproducible results.
